Our Current Campaign

Stop the normalisation
of strangling in
the media

We are calling on the European Broadcasting Union (EBU) and the BBC not to broadcast the Eurovision entry “Choke Me” unless its lyrics are changed. This song repeatedly references choking and strangulation - a form of violence overwhelmingly used against women. Broadcasting it risks normalising a dangerous and abusive act on one of Europe's largest stages.

Sign petition

Key campaign details

This includes raising awareness of the following: 1) Strangulation is disproportionately experienced by women.
2) It is increasingly experienced by young people in the context of sexual relationships
3) key facts of the standalone non-fatal strangulation law (in England Wales)
4) there is no safe way to strangle
5) ‘choking’ should not be used when the correct term is strangulation.
6) normalisation of strangulation contributes to harm

To begin with, we are targeting the BBC and upcoming Eurovision song contest which
will feature the song ‘Choke Me’.
